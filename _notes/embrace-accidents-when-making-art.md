When making art, if things go horribly wrong you should embrace it. Take the accident and reclaim it as something you meant to do. How does it change the art? Is it better after the accident? 

## References

- This was really evident in [[this-is-what-you-get-stanley-donwood-and-thom-yorke-exhibition]], where [[Stanley-Donwood]] spilt oil over detailed studies, resulting in the cover of *In Rainbows*. 
- [[Ralph-Steadman]]'s work is built from these accidents
